Introduction


This article’s goal is to give students a quick self-assessment for their English level.

It consists of five levels.

Each section gives a quick description of what the students can do at this level

Followed by what the students should be focusing on when studying at this level.

Followed by some recommendations of resources and activities.


Beginner (Vocabulary And Pronunciation )


Beginner students need to focus on building their vocabulary, as a result they cannot string a lot of sentences together because they do not have the vocabulary.

They could walk into a coffee shop and say…

“That please”

Or

“This please”

When you are traveling abroad for the first time, and you learn some of the language to help you get by this is the beginner level.

Students at this level should not focus on learning any grammar

Why?

They don’t have enough vocabulary to put the words they have into sentences.

Students at this level need to do two things…

1) Focus on learning the most common vocabulary (start at the most common 300 words and then work your way up to the most common 1000) 2) Focus on the pronunciation of minimal pairs (the sounds in English that sound the same [bitch and beach, think and sink,)

For minimal pairs check out this website, it has all the minimal pairs in English, use your teacher to correct your pronunciation and avoid getting into bad habit in the future.

For vocabulary check out this website

It only focuses on the most common nouns and adjectives, the things that you will need at this level

You will also need to be able to practice and remember this vocabulary effectively so check out…

1)How to make better flashcards

2) When to practice what you have learned according to neuroscience.


Upper-Beginner (Grammar)


Upper-beginner students should now start to focus on learning the grammar, while still focusing on the most common vocabulary in English (the 2000 most common words).

Students at this level can talk about everyday topics

1) Asking for directions 2) Ordering from a menu 3) Brief conversations about movies and music that they enjoy or do not like

Although students at this level can string simple sentences together however, they have trouble with the different tenses and most of their mistakes will be related to the grammar they are using.

The trick to improve your grammar quickly is to focus on one thing at a time before moving onto the next (an also make sure you are both producing and absorbing the language)

A great article that tells you how to do this is here, which is great for getting started.

However, this activity alone will not help student improve their speaking and listening.

So to improve your speaking check out this article which builds on the last activity but explains how to do this with your teacher.

Finally, one thing that students cannot ignore is their listening, if this falls behind then there will be problems at a later date.

So check out our activity for how to improve your listening speed here and this activity about transcribing your listening.


Intermediate (Most Students Stop Here) [Work and IELTS]


Up to now, students can follow the planned-out path in textbooks.

Why?

You are working with the most common vocabulary and topics.

Check this out

When you understand the 1000 most common words you will understand around 80% of most normal conversations.

2000 will get you to around 90% and 5000 will get you to around 95%.

12,000… which is what they recommend you need to go to an English speaking university is only 97%.

It is impossible to get to 100% comprehension (think of all the slang and specialist vocabulary from topics you are not interested in.

Once you get to intermediate level, students need to start studying topics in English that they need to use often… Improving their comprehension in these topics before moving onto the next topic.

Think about this a student who needs to communicate about digital marketing to different team members, clients and contribute in meetings and keep up to date about the latest marketing trends.

Compare this to a phd physics student who is researching quantum mechanics.

This kind of independent studying can be a challenge for most.

So some of the activities that we recommend are

1) Reverse translation to test how you can express yourself. 2) Intensive reading to learn the vocabulary you need to in context

Most importantly you need to be able to practice what you are learning when speaking.


Upper-Intermediate (optional) [Getting Your Accent]


This stage is optional

This stage is where students start to develop their accents

Important: There is a difference between good pronunciation and developing your accent. If you can get your minimal pairs correct, then your accent is clean.

But some students would like to have an American Accent or A English Accent.

This stage has much more to do with immersing yourself in one specific type of English (English, American, Australian)

Tip: The best way to approach this method is to understand the culture… find out what they find is funny and why they find this funny.

Compare English Humour to American humour as an example.

Advanced (Specialists)


The hardest thing that I have seen a student do is to

1) Have a conversation on the phone (without video) 2) With a non-native speaker 3) Of a different nationality 4) Who is a lower level than you are

I’ve seen a lot of students who need to communicate with international teams about their work.

At this level, students do not need a native English teacher… they need to be practicing with the nationalities that they need to be speaking to the most.

If you are a tour guide and a lot of your customers are from Italy or Korea, you need to be practicing with this nationality.

Why?

Different nationalities make different pronunciation mistakes.

In Japan a famous case is not being able to say the R and L.

Tip: Practice with people who are of a lower level than you, because lower levels make more mistakes, and your job right now is to get used to their mistakes… this will allow you to be more effective with your phone conversations.
